Household Median Income

The source data was grouped by MCI and the household median income for all 44 wards to count the number of crimes for these 2 dimensions. The resulting dataframe was pivoted by the MCI to obtain a new dataframe with 44 rows (one for each ward) and 5 columns (one for each MCI). The pivoted dataframe was used to perform the Anova test which yielded a pvalue = 0.5710328536406233 by which we can conclude that there is no significant statistical difference between the median of crime occurrences across the wards (represented by their respective household median income).
